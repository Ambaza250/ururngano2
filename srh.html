Information yizewe
Ese ni iki nkwiriye kumenya mu gihe cy’imihango?

Ukwiye kumenya ko kujya mu mihango ari kimwe mu bimenyetso bigaragaza ko umukobwa arimo kuva mu kiciro cy’ubwana ajya mu bukuru. Ibi bigaragaza ko umubiri w’umukobwa ukora neza.

Imihango ni iki?

Imihango ni amaraso asohoka aturutse muri nyababyeyi agasohokera mu gitsina cy’umukobwa cyangwa umugore bitewe nuko nta sama ryabayeho muri uko kwezi.

  • Igihe cy’uruburumbuke, intanga ngore isohoka mu mirerantanga igaterereza intangangabo, muri icyo gihe nyababyeyi ikora imyiteguro yo kwakira urusoro, iyo hatabayeho guhura kw’intanga ngabo n’intanga ngore, imyiteguro ya nyababyeyi irasenuka, igasohoka mu ishusho y’amaraso ariyo mihango.
  • Ubusanzwe imihango imara hagati y’iminsi 3-5 muri rusange. Iyo minsi isohora kugabanuka cyangwa ikarenga ku bagore cyangwa abakobwa bamwe na bamwe. Iyo imihango irengeje iminsi irindwi, umukobwa cyangwa umugore asabwa kwihutira kujya kwa muganga bakamufasha.
  • Abakobwa bamwe bagira imihango ibabaza, ariko ntibivuga ko baba barwaye.

Ese umukobwa atangira kujya mu mihango ryari?

Kujya mu mihango bwa mbere bitangira hagati y’imyaka 12 na 14, ariko hari abashobora kubona imihango mbere y’icyo gihe nko ku myaka 9, 10 cyangwa nyuma nko ku myaka 16 bitewe n’imiterere y’umubiri.

Umwangavu asabwa gutinyuka agasobanuzwa ababyeyi, abaganga n’abandi babihuguriwe kugira ngo amenye uko yitwara.

Ni iki cyamfasha kumva no kumvisha abandi ko kujya mu mihango ari ibisanzwe mu buzima atari uburwayi?

Kujya mu mihango si uburwayi ahubwo ni ibintu bisanzwe bibaho mu buzima, kandi ni kimwe mu bigaragaza ko umukobwa cyangwa umugore afite ubuzima bwiza.

  • Kugirana ku mihango mu buryo bwiza
  • Kwirinda kuvuga imihango uyivugira mu migani cyangwa amagambo azimije
  • Guharanira kugira uburenganzira bwo kubona ibikoresho by’isuku mu gihe cy’imihango
  • Guharanira gutegura neza ukoresheje twagufasha mu gihe cy’imihango aho ukorera cyangwa wiga
  • Hangana n’uru rwego rw’isebanya bihabwa abari mu mihango.

Ni byiza kwirinda guha akato no gusebyana abakobwa na bagore bari mu mihango, tukamenya ko imiterere ya baganzi bacye idakwiriye kuba imbarutso yo kubahutaza ahubwo ariyo gihe gikwiye cyo kubafasha no kubaba hafi, tukabaka umuryango wubahana kandi ushyigikira ihame ry’uburinganire n’ubwuzuzanye muri byose.

Ubutumwa bw’inyongera:
  • Kujya mu mihango si uburwayi ahubwo ni ubuzima kandi n’ikimenyetso cy’uko umwangavu yinjiye mu gihe cy’ubukure, bikanagaragaza ko umukobwa cyangwa umugore afite ubuzima bwiza.
  • Ni byiza kumenya gukurikirana ukwezi kwe k’umugore, ukamenya igihe imihango isohora kuzira, kugira ngo bikurinde gutungurwa n’imihango.
  • Iyo umukobwa yatangiye kubona imihango bigaragaza ko yakura ashobora gusama mu gihe akoze imibonano mpuzabitsina idakingiye.
  • Igihe hari ibibazo udasobanukiwe ku bijyanye n’imihango, irinde ibihuha ahubwo ugane ahatangirwa amakuru yizewe n’indege n’itandukanye ku bijyanye n’ubuzima bw’imyororokere, nko ku kigonderabuzima, ikigo cy’urubyiruko cyangwa ubundi bufasha buboneka.
Ese ni ibihe bimenyetso bishobora kunyereka ko ngiye kujya mu mihango?

Impinduka nyinshi z’umubiri n’amarangamutima zibaho ku mukobwa cyangwa umugore mbere yo kujya mu mihango kandi zigagaragara mu buryo bunyuranye kuri buri muntu.

  • Ibimenyetso bibanziriza imihango akenshi bitangira mu cyumweru kimwe cyangwa bibiri mbere yo kujya mu mihango, bikarangira mu minsi ine nyuma yo kujya mu mihango.
  • Hari ibimenyetso bitandukanye bibanziriza imihango, birimo ibigaragara inyuma ndetse n’ibyo mu mitekerereze utabonesha amaso bikagaragarira mu myitwarire.
  • Ni ngombwa kwibuka ko ushobora kugira bimwe cyangwa ukagira bike muri byo bitewe n’imikorere y’umubiri wawe.

Ibimenyetso byo k’umubiri nko;

  • Kubabara/kuribwa umutwe
  • Kubabara mu kiziba cy’inda
  • Kubabara no kubyimba amabere, ndetse n’uburyaryate ku imoko
  • Gutura umubi
  • Kugira umunaniro
  • Kuzana ighieri cyangwa ibihe mu maso, mu mugongo, n’ahandi.
  • Kumva udashaka kurya, cyangwa ushaka kurya cyane birenze uko wari usanzwe urya n’ibindi.

Ibimenyetso bijyanye n’imitekerereze;

  • Kumva ubabaye cyangwa udatuje
  • Kumva udashaka ibintu bimwe na bimwe
  • Kubura ibitotsi cyangwa kumva ushaka kuryamira kubera umunaniro
  • Impinduka mu byiyumviro byo gukora imibonano mpuzabitsina
  • Kugorwa no gufata umwanzuro ngenderwaho n’ibindi

Ibimenyetso bibanziriza imihango ntabwo ari uburwayi ni ibintu bisanzwe. Ibi biba ikibazo iyo ibimenyetso bigaragaza impinduka zidasanzwe mu mibereho yawe ya buri munsi nko kubabara bikabije, kumva utishimye n’ibindi. Igihe ibyo bimenyetso bihutira kujya kwa muganga kugira ngo ufashwe hakiri kare.

Ni iki cyagufasha guhangana cyangwa kugabanya ibimenyetso bibanziriza imihango?
  • Gerageza gukora imyitozo ngororamubiri, byibura iminota 30 buri munsi, ushobora kugenda n’amaguru, kwiruka, kunyonga igare, n’ibindi
  • Kurya indyo yuzuye, ihagije kandi iteguranye isuku, irimo ibiyubaka umubiri nk’inyama, amata n’amagi, ibitera imbaraga nk’umuceri, ibijumba ndetse n’ibirinda indwara nk’imboga n’imbuto.
  • Gushaka uburyo bwo kuruhuka nko gukora yoga no guhumeka cyane birafasha.
  • Gerageza uryame uruhuke bihagije, byibura amasaha umunani k’umunsi.
Ubutumwa bw’inyongera:
  • Ibimenyetso by’imihango birasanzwe ntibivuze ko ari uburwayi.
  • Ibimenyetso bibanziriza imihango ntabwo ari rusange ku bakobwa cyangwa abagore bose, bigenda biba bitandukanye bitewe n’imiterere y’umubiri w’umuntu ndetse n’imibereho ye.
  • Igihe cyose ugize ikibazo kijyanye n’imihango, ni byiza kwegera umubyeyi, umurezi cyangwa inama cyangwa ukagana kwa muganga hakarebwa bakagufasha.
Ese ni iki nakora ngo ngabanye uburibwe mu gihe cy'imihango?

Kuri bwa munda mu gihe cy’imihango ni ibintu bisanzwe ritabwo ari uburwayi ariko igihe ububare butihanganirwa kugeraho bituma udakora inshingano zawe za buri munsi, ihutire kuya kwa muganga bagufashe banakugabanyirize ububabare.

Bimwe mubyagufasha guhangana n’ububabare mugiye cy’imihango harimo:

  • Gukoresha amazi ashyushye mu kwivura: Gushyira ubushyuhe ku nda bishobora kugufasha koroshya imikaya iri ku kurya bikaba byagabanya kubabara. Dore uko bikora: Koresha agakoresho kabugenewe, cyangwa icupa ririmo amazi ashyushye (adatwika), ushyireho agatambaro ku nda ahagana hejuru ku kiziba kinda, hanyuma urambikeho iryo cupa ririmo amazi ashyushye mu gihe cy’ iminota 15-20. Ushobora kandi gukoresha igitambaro cy’amazi (isume) ishyushye(idatwika) ukayishyira ku kiziba kinda mu gihe cy’iminota 15-20.
  • Koga Amazi Ashyushye: Koga amazi ashyushye bishobora gutuma umubiri wawe umera neza bikaba byagabanya uburibwe.
  • Imyitozo ngororamubiri yoroheje: Gukora imyitozo ngororamubiri yoroheje, nko kugendagenda, kwigorora (Yoga) n’ibindi bishobora gufasha amaraso gutembera neza bikagabanya uburibwe. Irinde gukora imyitozo ngororamubiri ikomeye mu gihe uri kuribwa kubera imihango.
  • Kurya indyo yuzuye: Ni byiza kurya indyo yuzuye kuko bifasha umubiri gukora neza. Ibyo kurya n’ibyo kunywa bimwe bishobora konger a uburibwe mu gihe cy’imihango, niyo mpamvu ari ngombwa guhagarika kunywa ikawa nyinshi, inzoga n’ibinyobwa umunyu mwinshi kuko bishobora gutera kugugara bikaba byatuma uribwa.
  • Kwirinda umwuma: Kunywa amazi ni ingenzi mu gihe uri mu mihango kuko bishobora kugabanya kugugari rwa mu nda ndetse no kuribwa. Nywa amazi menshi ashoboka, byibura litro ebyiri ku munsi, kandi wirinde ibintu birim o umunyu mwinshi cyangwa ibirimo ikawa kuko bishobora gutuma umubiri ugira umwuma.
  • Gukoresha uburyo bwo kuruhuka: Kuruhuka no gukora umwitozo wo guhumeka byimbitse (deep breath), gutekereza byimbitse (Meditation) no guhugira mu bigoyeye amaso nko kureba amashusho bishobora kugabanya umuhangayiko (stress) ishobora gutuma uburibwe bw’imihango bwyongera. Fata umwanya wiyiteho kandi uruhuke bihagije.
  • Gukanda ahantu habugenewe buhoro buhoro (massage): Gukanda ahantu habugenewe mu kubiri nka hagati y’igikunira na mukubitarukoko, cyangwa ku kiziba cy’inda, ukahakanda buhoro buhoro ukora uruziga (umasa) mu gihe cy’ iminota mike bishobora kugabanya ububabare mu gihe cy’imihango.
Ubutumwa bw’inyongera:

Ni ingenzi ko umenya umubiri wawe, ukamenya uburyo bwagufasha mu kugabanya ububabare bwo mugiye cy’imihango kuko uburyo bumwe bushobora gukora ku muntu umwe ariko ntibukore ku wundi.

Kuribwa mu gihe cy’imihango ntabwo ari uburwayi, gusa niba ubabare bukabije bugatuma utabasha inshingano zawe, ihutire gusaba ubufasha ku ivuriro rikwegereye kuko wabishobora neza.

Ni ryari umukobwa cyangwa umugore aba afite amahirwe menshi yo gusama?

Umukobwa cyangwa umugore aba afite amahirwe menshi yo gusama kurenza ibindi bihe, iyo ari mu gihe cy’uburumbuke.

Uburumbuke ni iki?

Uburumbuke k’umukobwa cyangwa umugore ni igihe cy’ishisha n’irekurwa ry’intangangore(igi) riva mu mirerantanga rijya mu miyoborantanga, akaba ari icyo gihe mu kwezi k’umugore/umukobwa aba afite amahirwe menshi yo gusama aramutse akoze imibonano mpuzabitsina idakingiye.

Gusama kubaho iyo umukobwa/umugore akoranye imibonano mpuzabitsina idakingiye n’umugabo cyangwa umusore, intanga ngore igahura n’intanga ngabo bigakora urusoro, arirwo rukura rukavamo umwana.

Kureka kw’igi (intanga ngore) ni iki?

Kureka kw’igi ni kimwe mu bice bisanzwe bigize uburumbuke, muri iki gihe kamwe mu dusabo tw’intanga ngore (ak’iburyo cyangwa ibumoso) kareka igi (intangangore), ikajya mu miyobora ntanga aho ritegereza intanga ngabo.

Ni ryari igi rirekurwa?

Abakobwa cyangwa abagore bose baba abafite ukwezi guhindagurika ndetse n’ukudahindagurika, mu kwezi kwabo bagira igice kidahinduka kigizwe n’iminsi 14. Iki gice kibarwa duhereye inyuma, aho kiva k’umunsi wa nyuma w’ukwezi k’umugore kikagera k’umunsi wa mbere imbere mu ry’umukobwa/umugore ryarekuriwemo.

Ese uburumbuke bumara igihe kingana gute?

Uburumbuke ni igihe umukobwa aba afite amahirwe menshi yo gusama. N’ubwo iyo ntangangore nyuma yo kurekurwa n’agasabo k’intanga ishobora kumara amasaha 24 ikiri nzima itegereje mu miyobora ntanga, igihe uburumbuke bumara kirekire aya masha 24 kuko gusama bigirwamo uruhare n’intanga ngore ndetse n’intanga ngabo. Igihe uburumbuke bumara kigenwa n’ubwoko bw’ukwezi k’umugore.

Ubutumwa bw’inyongera:

Ni ngombwa kumenya ko mu kwezi k’umugore nta gihe nakiwe umuntu aba atasama bitewe n’imikorere ndetse n’imibonano mpuzabitsina y’umubiri.

N’ubwo gusama biberamo myanya myinshi y’umubiri, gusa biba byagizemwo uruhare n’intanga ngore ndetse n’intangangabo, akaba ariyo mpamvu iminsi y’uburumbuke iba ari myinshi ugereranyije n’igihe intangangore (igi) imara irekuwe itegerereje mu miyoborantanga.

Virusi itera SIDA (HIV)

1. Intangiriro

Virusi itera SIDA (VIH) iracyari ikibazo gikomeye ku rwego rw’isi mu by’ubuzima rusange, cyane cyane mu rubyiruko. Kubura amakuru ahagije n’imyitwarire ishobora gushyira umuntu mu kaga bituma urubyiruko rwibasirwa cyane.Uru rubuga rugamije gutanga ubumenyi busobanutse kuri VIH, uburyo yandura, ingaruka zayo ndetse n’ingamba zo kuyirinda, hagamijwe guteza imbere imibereho myiza y’urubyiruko.

2. Intego z’isomo

  • Gusobanura neza VIH na SIDA.
  • Kugaragaza uburyo VIH yanduramo cyane.
  • Kwerekana ingaruka VIH igira ku mubiri w’umuntu.
  • Kugaragaza uburyo bwo kwirinda VIH.
  • Gutanga inama zafasha urubyiruko kwirinda kwandura VIH.

3. Igisobanuro cya Virusi itera SIDA (VIH)

VIH ni virusi yibasira uturemangingo turinda umubiri (CD4), igatuma ubudahangarwa bugabanuka. Iyo itavuwe ishobora gutera SIDA.

4. Uburyo Virusi itera SIDA(VIH ) Yanduramo

  • Gukora imibonano mpuzabitsina idakingiye.
  • Gusangira ibikoresho bikomeretsa byanduye.
  • Kwakirirwa amaraso yanduye.
  • Kwanduzwa n’umubyeyi ujya ku mwana mugihe avuka cyangwa amutwite.

Icyitonderwa: VIH ntayandurira mu gusuhuzanya cyangwa gusangira amafunguro.

5. Ingaruka za Virusi itera SIDA( VIH)

  • Indwara zifata umubiri byoroshye.
  • Umuriro n’umunaniro.
  • Gutakaza ibiro.
  • Impiswi zidakira.
  • Ibisebe by’uruhu.
  • SIDA ishobora gutera urupfu.

6. Uburyo bwo Kwirinda Virusi itera SIDA( VIH)

  • Kwifata.
  • Ubudahemuka.
  • Kwirinda imibonano mpuzabitsina idakingiye.
  • Gukoresha agakingirizo.
  • Kugira umukunzi umwe.
  • Kwirinda gusangira ibikoresho bikomeretsa nk'inzembe,imikasi n'ibindi.
  • Kwipimisha VIH inshuro nyinshi kugira ngo bamenye uko ubuzima bwabo buhagaze.

7. Inama zigenewe Urubyiruko

  • Kwipimisha VIH inshuro nyinshi kugira ngo bamenye uko ubuzima bwabo buhagaze.
  • Kwirinda igitutu cy’urungano ndetse n'ibigare.
  • Gufata ibyemezo bizima bigendanye n'ubuzima bwite.
  • Kwibanda ku masomo n'ibindi bibafitiye akamaro.
  • Gushaka amakuru yizewe ndetse no kugana inzobere Aho bikenewe.
  • Kwirinda ivangura iryo ariryo ryose.
8. Umwanzuro

VIH iririndwa kandi uburezi ni ingenzi mu kuyikumira.Urubyiruko rugirwa inama yo kwirinda icyo aricyo cyose cyarushora mu mibonano mpuzabitsina imburagihe.Ndetse no kwirinda igitutu cy'urungano n'ikigare bibashora mu myanzuro batatekerejeho.

What should I know during menstruation?

You should know that having your period is one of the signs that a girl is leaving childhood and entering adulthood. This shows that the girl’s body is functioning well.

What is menstruation?

Menstruation is blood that comes from the uterus and exits through the vagina of a girl or woman because no fertilization occurred that month.

  • During the ovulation period, the egg is released from the ovaries to meet the sperm. At that time the uterus prepares to receive the fertilized egg. If the sperm and egg do not meet, the uterus lining sheds and comes out as blood — that is menstruation.
  • Normally, menstruation lasts between 3-5 days on average. For some girls and women it can be shorter or longer. If menstruation lasts more than 7 days, the girl or woman should see a doctor immediately.
  • Some girls experience painful periods, but this does not mean they are sick.

When does a girl start menstruating?

The first period usually starts between the ages of 12 and 14, but some may start as early as 9 or 10, or as late as 16, depending on the body.

A young person should feel free to ask parents, doctors or trained people so they know how to behave.

What can help me understand and explain to others that menstruation is normal and not a disease?

Menstruation is not a disease but a normal part of life, and it is one of the signs that a girl or woman has good health.

  • Talk about menstruation in a positive way
  • Avoid talking about menstruation using metaphors or negative words
  • Advocate for the right to access sanitary products during menstruation
  • Plan well by using sanitary products where you work or study
  • Fight against the stigma faced by people who are menstruating.

It is good to avoid stigmatizing and insulting girls and women who are menstruating. We should know that the natural changes in a few friends should not be a reason to harass them, but rather the right time to support them and stay close to them. Let us build a respectful family that supports the principle of equality and complementarity in everything.

Additional Message:
  • Menstruation is not a disease but a sign of good health and that a young person has entered adulthood.
  • It is good to track your menstrual cycle so you know when your period is due and avoid being surprised.
  • When a girl starts menstruating, it shows she has grown and can become pregnant if she has unprotected sex.
  • If you have questions you don’t understand about menstruation, avoid rumours and go to trusted sources of information on reproductive health such as health centres, youth centres or other available support.
What are the signs that I may be about to have my period?

Many physical and emotional changes occur in a girl or woman before menstruation and they appear differently in every person.

  • Premenstrual symptoms often start one or two weeks before the period and end four days after the period.
  • There are various premenstrual symptoms, some visible on the outside and others that cannot be seen with the eyes but appear in behaviour.
  • It is important to remember that you may have some or few of them depending on how your body works.

Physical symptoms include:

  • Headache
  • Abdominal cramps
  • Breast pain and swelling, and nipple sensitivity
  • Feeling bloated
  • Fatigue
  • Swelling of the face, back and other areas.
  • Loss of appetite or increased appetite more than usual.

Emotional / mental symptoms:

  • Feeling sad or restless
  • Losing interest in some things
  • Insomnia or wanting to sleep a lot because of fatigue
  • Changes in sexual desire
  • Difficulty making decisions and others

Premenstrual symptoms are not a disease; they are normal. They become a problem when the symptoms show unusual changes in your daily life such as severe pain or feeling unhappy. If these symptoms become intense, see a doctor early for help.

What can help you cope with or reduce premenstrual symptoms?
  • Try to do light physical exercise, at least 30 minutes every day — walking, running, cycling, etc.
  • Eat a balanced, sufficient and hygienic diet that includes body-building foods like meat, milk and eggs, energy-giving foods like rice and potatoes, and disease-preventing foods like vegetables and fruits.
  • Find ways to relax such as doing yoga and deep breathing.
  • Try to sleep enough, at least eight hours a day.
Additional Message:
  • Menstrual symptoms are normal and do not mean you are sick.
  • Premenstrual symptoms are not the same for all girls or women; they vary depending on the person’s body and lifestyle.
  • Whenever you have a problem related to menstruation, it is good to approach a parent, guardian, counsellor or go to a doctor early so they can help you.
What can I do to reduce pain during menstruation?

Abdominal pain during menstruation is normal and is not a disease, but when the pain is unbearable and prevents you from doing your daily duties, hurry to the doctor so they can help you and reduce the pain.

Some things that can help you cope with menstrual pain include:

  • Using warm water for a hot compress: Applying heat to the abdomen can help relax the muscles around the uterus and reduce pain. How it works: Use a special hot water bottle or a bottle filled with warm (not burning) water, place a cloth on the lower abdomen, then place the warm bottle on it for 15-20 minutes. You can also use a warm cloth (towel) and place it on the lower abdomen for 15-20 minutes.
  • Taking a warm bath: Taking a warm bath can make your body feel better and reduce pain.
  • Light physical exercise: Doing light exercises such as walking, stretching (Yoga) and others can help blood flow better and reduce pain. Avoid intense exercise when you have menstrual pain.
  • Eating a balanced diet: It is good to eat a balanced diet because it helps the body function well. Some foods and drinks can increase pain during menstruation, which is why it is important to stop drinking too much coffee, alcohol and salty drinks because they can cause bloating and make you feel more pain.
  • Avoiding dehydration: Drinking water is very important during menstruation because it can reduce abdominal bloating and pain. Drink as much water as possible, at least two litres a day, and avoid foods with too much salt or coffee because they can make the body dehydrated.
  • Using relaxation techniques: Resting and doing deep breathing exercises, meditation and closing your eyes to watch videos can reduce stress that can increase menstrual pain. Take time for yourself and rest enough.
  • Gentle massage on specific areas: Gently massaging specific areas of the body such as between the pubic bone and the lower back, or the lower abdomen, making circular movements (massage) for a few minutes can reduce pain during menstruation.
Additional Message:

It is important to know your body and know what works for you to reduce menstrual pain because one method may work for one person but not for another.

Menstrual pain is not a disease, but if the pain is too severe and prevents you from doing your duties, hurry to ask for help at the nearest health centre because you can be helped.

When does a girl or woman have the highest chance of getting pregnant?

A girl or woman has the highest chance of getting pregnant compared to other times when she is in the ovulation period.

What is ovulation?

Ovulation in a girl or woman is the time when the egg is released from the ovaries into the fallopian tubes. This is the time in a woman’s or girl’s monthly cycle when she has the highest chance of getting pregnant if she has unprotected sex.

Pregnancy occurs when a girl or woman has unprotected sex with a man or boy, the egg meets the sperm and they form a fertilized egg, which develops into a baby.

What is the release of the egg?

The release of the egg is one of the normal parts of ovulation. In this time, one of the small sacs in the ovary (right or left) releases the egg, which travels into the fallopian tube where it waits for the sperm.

When is the egg released?

All girls or women who have regular or irregular periods have a fixed 14-day phase in their cycle. This phase is counted backwards from the last day of the woman’s period to the first day before the egg is released.

How long does ovulation last?

Ovulation is the time when a girl has the highest chance of getting pregnant. Although the egg can survive for up to 24 hours after being released, the ovulation window is longer (about 24 hours) because pregnancy also depends on both the egg and the sperm. The length of the ovulation period depends on the type of menstrual cycle.

Additional Message:

It is important to know that in a woman’s monthly cycle there is no time when a person cannot get pregnant because of the way the body and sexual intercourse work.

Although pregnancy can happen in many parts of the body, it mainly involves the egg and the sperm, which is why the ovulation days are more than the time the egg stays waiting in the fallopian tube.

HIV / AIDS Virus

1. Introduction

The HIV virus that causes AIDS is still a serious global health problem, especially among young people. Lack of enough information and risky behaviours put young people at high risk. This platform aims to provide clear knowledge about HIV, how it is transmitted, its effects and ways to prevent it, with the goal of improving the well-being of young people.

2. Lesson Objectives

  • To clearly explain HIV and AIDS.
  • To show the main ways HIV is transmitted.
  • To show the effects of HIV on the human body.
  • To show ways to prevent HIV.
  • To give advice that helps young people avoid getting infected with HIV.

3. Explanation of the HIV Virus

HIV is a virus that attacks the immune cells (CD4) that protect the body, causing the immune system to weaken. If not treated, it can lead to AIDS.

4. How the HIV Virus is Transmitted

  • Having unprotected sex.
  • Sharing contaminated sharp objects.
  • Receiving infected blood.
  • Being infected from mother to child during birth or breastfeeding.

Note: HIV is not transmitted through shaking hands or sharing food.

5. Effects of the HIV Virus

  • Diseases that easily affect the body.
  • Fever and fatigue.
  • Weight loss.
  • Non-healing wounds.
  • Skin sores.
  • AIDS can cause death.

6. Ways to Prevent the HIV Virus

  • Abstinence.
  • Faithfulness.
  • Avoiding unprotected sex.
  • Using condoms.
  • Having only one partner.
  • Avoiding sharing sharp objects like razors, scissors, etc.
  • Getting tested for HIV regularly to know your health status.

7. Advice for Young People

  • Get tested for HIV regularly to know your health status.
  • Avoid peer pressure and bad groups.
  • Make healthy decisions that match your personal life.
  • Focus on studies and other useful activities.
  • Seek reliable information and consult experts when needed.
  • Avoid any form of discrimination.
8. Conclusion

HIV can be prevented and education is very important in stopping it. Young people are advised to avoid anything that could lead them into unprotected sex early. They should also avoid peer pressure and bad groups that can lead them to decisions they have not thought about.
